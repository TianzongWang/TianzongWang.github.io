  • Information Share and Model Averaging, with Stuart Hyde and Sungjun Cho

    • This paper proposes a new methodology to calculate the information share (IS) with model averaging (MA) methodologies. Specifically, we consider the AIC and BIC model averaging, and the Jackknife model averaging (JMA). Our experiment shows that when the true model is unknown, MA can significantly increase the accuracy of IS and other variable estimations, e.g. latent efficient price series.

Working Progress

  • Stock Market Simulator, with Jorge Cruz Lopez

    • We develop a new framework to simulate centralized equity markets intraday, incorporating investors with diverse expectation formation and trading strategies. The simulator provides insights into how the proportions and interactions of different investor types can lead to varying realized prices and enables the assessment of how different market scenarios and shocks impact realized prices, investor behaviors, asset holdings, and cash balances.

  • Cross-Section Signal vs. Endogenous Market Microstructure Variables, Solo Paper

    • I examine the contribution proportion of cross-section sparse signals of return and various endogenous market microstructure variables across frequencies by several machine learning models. Furthermore, with the a priori refined signal variable set, I propose a rolling forecasting framework across frequencies.

  • Beyond Sentiment, Machine Learning on Reports, Solo Paper

    • Investigate to what extent the incremental benefits of various large language models from dictionary indicators, derived from 10-K reports on stock return prediction, can be attributed to other variables within the report.
